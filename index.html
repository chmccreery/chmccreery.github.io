Clara McCreery

mccreery at cs dot stanford dot edu

I am a masters student in computer science at Stanford University specializing in artificial intelligence. My research, advised by Professor Christopher Ré, focuses on weak supervision, multi-task learning, and applications to genomics.

I was a course assistant for CS110: Principles of Computer Systems, the content of which includes filesystems, multi-processing, threading, signal handling, and distributed computing. In this course, students implement a terminal shell, web proxy, and map-reduce using C and C++.

Previous final projects have included teaching a simulated robot to copy a digit after only looking at one example of that digit and performing DNA sequence denoising.
