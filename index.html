Clara McCreery

mccreery at cs dot stanford dot edu

I am a master's student in computer science at Stanford University, specializing in artificial intelligence.

Projects

My research has mostly focused on weak supervision, multi-task learning, and applications to medicine. Last summer, as a machine learning research intern at Curai, I focused on detecting similar medical questions in order to make online medical care more scalable and affordable. I will be presenting that research at the ML4H workshop at NeurIPS in December 2019.

This past year I was also part of a team of graduate students that achieved a state-of-the-art score on the GLUE Benchmark. In school, I have done a number of independent projects in machine learning as part of my coursework.

Prior to my graduate studies, I worked at MIT Lincoln Laboratory for 3 years on computer vision and data visualization. Most of my work there was geared toward defense against chemical, biological, nuclear, and explosive threats.

Teaching

As a course assistant for CS110: Principles of Computer Systems, I have taught students about filesystems, multi-processing, threading, signal handling, and distributed computing. Example assignments included implementing a terminal shell, ptrace, a web proxy, and map-reduce using C and C++.

Other teaching experience includes general chemistry at Stanford (Chem 31x) and volunteer tutoring with children who are learning to read.
