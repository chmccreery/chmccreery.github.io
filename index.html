Machine Learning Projects

I am a master's student in computer science at Stanford University, specializing in artificial intelligence. My research, advised by Professor Christopher Ré, focuses on weak supervision, multi-task learning, and applications to genomics.

Many of the classes I have taken included independent final projects in machine learning. For CS221: Artificial Intelligence, a partner and I trained a network to take sequential actions to copy a digit after only looking at one example of that digit. For CS230: Deep Learning, I worked with two other students to train a network that could recognize and remove noise from DNA sequences. We implemented and compared CNN and LSTM architectures for this application. For CS231A: I focused on pixel-wise segmentation of a smoke plume from an image. I was interested in using the resulting segmentations to reconstruct amorphous, gaseous plumes in 3D.

Prior to becoming a graduate student, I worked at MIT Lincoln Laboratory for 3 years.

Teaching Experience

As a course assistant for CS110: Principles of Computer Systems, I taught students about filesystems, multi-processing, threading, signal handling, and distributed computing. Example assignments included implementing a terminal shell, ptrace, a web proxy, and map-reduce using C and C++. As an undergraduate, I was also a teaching assistant for general chemistry.
